Knowing Your Beer

Ale vs. Lager

A beer is either classed as an Ale or a Lager based on where the yeast settles during fermentation. A lager is classified as anything that uses bottom fermentation. This means that the yeast settles at the bottom after fermentation. The process of fermenting lagers is slower and they usually have a lighter taste and lower alcohol content than ales.

  • Common lagers are:
  • Heinekein - Pale Lager
  • Budweiser - American Lager
  • Guinness Black Lager - Black Lager

An ale, on the other hand, is brewed through what is called top fermentation. Instead of settling at the bottom of the beer during fermentation, the yeast settles at the top. Ales are often known for their full bodied and hoppier/bitter taste. Ales usually have a higher alchol content.

  • Common ales are:
  • Perennial Southside Blonde (89)- Belgian Style Beer
  • Lagunitas Imperial Red Ale - Imperial Red Ale
  • Dogfish Head 60 Minute IPA - Indian Pale Ale (IPA)

There is one more additional category for how yeast may settle during fermentation and that is through spontaneous fermentation.

Ales

Indian Pale Ale (IPA)

Originating from England in the 18th Century, when sailors needed a beer that could last on long journeys at sea without needing to be preserved, the IPA was born and has now become a staple within the craft beer community. IPAs are a pretty popular beer due to their variety of styles and flavor. Most notably known for its high alchol content and often mentioned hoppy or bitter flavor. IPAs usually have an earthy and malty aroma, but they can also include citrus tones. Their colors can range from golden to amber and come with a full head. Usually a clear body, but can also come as unfiltered otherwise known as "hazy". There are many different kinds of IPAs such as: American-style IPA (West Coast, East Coast, New England), English-style IPA, and the Double or Imperial IPA.

Wild & Sour Ales

Sour Ale

Our personal favorite at Beer Jou, is the Sour Ale. If you like a beer that has fruit notes, citrus flavors, and is light and tart, then this is the beer for you. There is an excellent balance between acidity and sweetness in the beer.

Known as the oldest beer due to its brewing process, sour ales are made with complex flavors and tones. To create a sour ale, they are fermented using sour ale yeast and varying types of bacteria such as Lactobacillus and Pediococcus. Common sour ales are: Berliner Weisse, Gose, Lambic beers, Flanders, American Wile Ales.
